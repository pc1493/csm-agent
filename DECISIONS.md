## #001 — Framework-free orchestration
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** Orchestrate the agent loop with plain Python functions in
`pipeline/orchestrator.py`. No LangGraph, CrewAI, or similar.
**Rationale:** The build is presented live to a panel grading critical thinking. A framework
hides the control flow that is exactly what they want to see, and adds learning/debugging
overhead under a 2-day clock. Plain Python is transparent, debuggable on a projector, and
demonstrates understanding of the loop rather than reliance on a black box. Defensible line:
"framework-free for transparency and control."

## #002 — DuckDB only; no dbt
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** Use a single local DuckDB file (`data/renewal.duckdb`) as the store. No dbt.
**Rationale:** Data volume is ~20 synthetic rows. dbt adds project ceremony (sources,
staging/marts, profiles) for zero analytical benefit at this scale, and steals hours from the
reliability layer, which is the differentiator. DuckDB matches the author's prior project and
is embedded, zero-server, instant.

## #003 — 0.75 confidence threshold for auto-vs-escalate
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** Analysis with `confidence >= 0.75` and no conflict/exception is auto-handled;
otherwise the account escalates to a human queue. Constant lives in
`agents/reliability.py::CONFIDENCE_THRESHOLD`.
**Rationale:** Mirrors the auto-classification threshold pattern from the author's email
project. 0.75 is a deliberately conservative starting point for a customer-facing action —
better to over-escalate early and relax with observed precision. The number is a knob, not a
law; revisit with calibration data.

## #004 — $5,000 upsell escalation threshold (the smart threshold)
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** If an account shows a genuine upsell signal with an estimated value
>= $5,000, escalate it to the AE queue **even though its ARR is under $20k** (exception E7).
**Rationale:** The naive rule is "low ARR = low priority = full automation." That misses the
small, happy account with a real expansion need worth multiples of its current spend. Routing
these to a human despite low ARR is the BA insight the panel is probing — value at stake, not
current revenue, drives the human's attention.

## #005 — No-response escalation after 3 touches
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** An account that does not reply after 3 outreach touches is flagged to the
`manual_outreach` queue (exception E3). Constant `NO_RESPONSE_TOUCHES`.
**Rationale:** Silence is signal but not actionable by the agent; a human should attempt a
different channel before the renewal lapses. 3 balances persistence against annoyance for a
low-touch segment.

## #006 — Single model (Sonnet 4.6) across all agents
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** All agents call `claude-sonnet-4-6`. (`agents/llm.py::MODEL`.)
**Rationale:** Predictable token cost and simpler debugging for a 2-day prototype. Documented
production optimization (not built): route persona-generation and first-pass triage to Haiku
and reserve Sonnet for low-confidence re-analysis — a cost lever that's on-theme for a
cost-driven use case. Noted here so the *thinking* is visible without spending the tokens.

## #007 — Simulated customers with hidden ground truth
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** Customer replies are generated by an LLM persona seeded with a hidden
`GroundTruth` (disposition, sentiment, reason, upsell fit). The analysis agent never sees it;
the eval scores against it.
**Rationale:** Makes the loop fully self-contained (no email/CRM dependency, demos live) and,
as a bonus, yields ground truth we'd never have in production — which is what makes the
accuracy/calibration number real. The seam is stated openly: in production this is replaced by
sampled human QA + renewal-outcome backtesting; the mechanism is unchanged.

## #008 — Autonomous-by-default with human escalation
**Status:** Active
**Decision:** The agent acts autonomously on clean, confident, non-conflicting cases and
escalates the rest. It is NOT a co-pilot that asks a human to approve every action.
**Rationale:** The entire business case is that humans are too expensive for this tail —
a human-approves-everything design defeats the cost rationale. The sophistication is the
*guardrail*: autonomy is earned per-account by confidence + absence of exceptions, and the
scarce human is spent only where it matters (detractors, real expansion dollars, low confidence).
